# Microbial-Systems-Analysis
Auditing the molecular logic of extremophiles using public genomic data.
# Microbial Systems Portfolio: Deinococcus radiodurans
**Focus:** Genomic Integrity & DNA Repair Mechanisms

## 🧬 Project Overview
An audit of the polyextremophile *D. radiodurans* (R1) to verify the digital representation of its radiation-resistance mechanisms using public datasets (NCBI BioProject: 234829).

---

## 🔍 Milestone 1: Genomic Quality Audit
**Tool:** QUAST (Quality Assessment Tool for Genome Assemblies)
**Dataset:** GCF_000008565.1 (RefSeq)

* **Observation:** Verified a **GC Content of 66.78%**. 
* **Logical Inference:** The high GC-ratio (3 hydrogen bonds vs 2 in AT pairs) provides the thermal and structural stability required for survival in extreme environments.
* **Assembly Health:** N50 of 140,393 bp, confirming a high-quality scaffold for functional annotation.

## 🛠️ Milestone 2: The Repair Engine Discovery
**Tool:** Prokka (Rapid Annotation) & NCBI BLASTp

* **The Problem:** Initial automated annotation (Prokka) failed to explicitly label the primary recombinase (RecA).
* **The Logic:** Identified associated "Loader" proteins (**RecF, RecO**) and specialized repair enzymes (**RadA, MutL, MutS**).
* **The Manual Curation:** Performed a manual BLASTp search of the "DNA strand exchange" protein. 
* **Verification:** Confirmed a **367aa RecA protein** with 100% homology to the *D. radiodurans* reference strain, overriding the automated gap.

## 🧱 Milestone 3: Structural Mechanism
**Tool:** PDB (Protein Data Bank)
**ID:** 1XP8
